2nd Quarter Exam

Speech

Good morning/afternoon everyone! My name is Rhian Praxides, and today I want to talk about discrimination—a force that continues to divide us and undermine the dignity of many, whether it's based on race, gender, or other factors.

How can we call ourselves human if we refuse to see humanity in others? No one is born to hate, yet we teach it through fear and ignorance. Discrimination steals opportunities, crushes dreams, and leaves scars that may never heal.

We are all different, but these differences make the world beautiful, not broken. Why should anyone feel less than another when we all share the same earth and breathe the same air?

Let us choose acceptance over prejudice, love over hate. Every small act of kindness is a step toward a better, fairer world. Together, we can break the chains of discrimination and build a future where everyone belongs.

Memorable Lines to Live By

Let us not be defined by the labels others place upon us. We are not divided by race, gender, or background—we are united by our shared humanity. Every act of kindness, no matter how small, ignites the spark of change. Let us rise above hate, rise above fear, and build a world where compassion reigns. A world where every individual feels seen, heard, and valued. The future is in our hands, and together, we can create a legacy of equality, love, and acceptance. Let's choose to be the change we wish to see, for in unity, there is strength, and in kindness, there is power.
